Traveler's Guide to Romania

Info:

  • Capital city is Bucharest.
  • Population of 19.4 million people.
  • Mainly Christian Orthodox.
  • Currency is the "leu" or "RON".
  • Climate: has 4 seasons like the Northeastern U.S.

Must See Locations:

  • Bucharest
  • Mamaia
  • Sinaia
  • Peles Castle
  • Mud Volcanoes

Bucharest

Bucharest, or Bucuresti as written in Romanian, is Romania's capital city. It has the government administration buildings, beatiful parks, museums, and (most interesting) "Museul Satului" which is a village of traditional Romanian houses across time. Just take a walk on the bustling streets and you'll fall in love.

Mamaia

If you like the beach, Mamaia is the town for you! Mamaia is a strip of beach on the Black Sea. It's beaches are great with spectacular hotels, restaurants, and attractions like water parks and cable cars over the city.

Sinaia

On the opposite end of the spectrum from the beach is Sinaia in the mountains. While it's a small town, its fresh air and friendly inhabitants make you feel truly free. Once you've enjoyed the wonders of the town like it's fairs and festivals, you can journey up to the mountain tops, where there are long plateaus with sheep and cows grazing. It truly is the experience of a lifetime!

Peles Castle

Peles Castle is situated in Sinaia and was constructed in 1873 for King Carol I. Since then, this Neo-Classical caslte has been the home of many famous Romanian kings and more recently, a very popular tourist destination. It's elegant and lavish rooms are truly a sight to behold!

Mud Volcanoes

Romania has a very intresting natural wonder called "Volcani Noroios" or Mud Volcanoes. Contrary to what you might be picturing, they are not huge structures that are driven by magmatic activity and emit lava. They are only a few feet high, and release mud and some gases. Nevertheless, they are truly mesmerizing to look at and are well worth a look on your next Romania visit!
